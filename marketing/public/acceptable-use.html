Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-21 · Part of the Terms of Service.

Drevalis Creator Studio is a tool for making content, full stop. We trust you to use it sensibly, and we don't read your prompts. But there's a short list of things we won't allow it to be used for: anything illegal, anything that hurts other people, and anything that breaks the rules of the platforms you upload to. Crossing those lines means we cancel your license — no refund.

You must not use the Software to create, upload, or distribute material that:

  • Sexualises minors. Any depiction of individuals under 18 in a sexual context is categorically prohibited. This includes photo-real, stylised, animated, or AI-generated depictions. We report credible evidence to the Swiss Federal Police Cybercrime Reporting (NCSC) and to the respective national authorities.
  • Impersonates real people without consent for the purpose of deceiving audiences (deepfake voice or image of a public figure or private person, passed off as authentic). Parody, satire, and commentary clearly labeled as such are permitted.
  • Incites violence or hatred against individuals or protected groups on the basis of race, ethnicity, religion, national origin, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, or similar characteristics (Swiss StGB Art. 261bis).
  • Defames identifiable individuals or businesses (Swiss StGB Art. 173 – 177) by presenting false claims of fact as if true.
  • Promotes self-harm or suicide, facilitates eating disorders, or targets vulnerable audiences with such material.
  • Infringes copyright or trademark rights at scale — e.g. re-voicing a copyrighted audiobook, reconstructing a film scene-for-scene, or mass-producing fan-fiction in a studio's protected style as if licensed.
  • Disseminates disinformation about elections, public-health emergencies, or other topics with a high public-interest stake, especially in a way designed to deceive the audience about authenticity.
  • Facilitates fraud or abuse: phishing scripts, fake testimonials attributed to identifiable people, investment scams, pump-and-dump promos, counterfeit-goods marketing.
  • Violates CSAM, terrorism, or export-control laws in your jurisdiction or in the jurisdiction of your audience.
  • Directly breaks the rules of the upload platform you use — e.g. YouTube's Spam, Deceptive Practices & Scams Policy, or TikTok's Integrity & Authenticity rules on synthetic media.

You must also not:

  • Use the Software's license system in a way it is not designed for — sharing your key, running it on more machines than your tier allows, or tampering with license-enforcement code;
  • Reverse-engineer, decompile, or extract the Software for the purpose of producing a derivative product;
  • Probe, stress-test, or attempt to exploit our license server or related infrastructure (this is unauthorised access under Swiss StGB Art. 143bis and constitutes a criminal offence);
  • Use the Software to send unsolicited bulk messages, scrape platforms beyond their rate limits, or bypass content moderation on the platforms you upload to.

Telling viewers your video is AI-made

YouTube and TikTok require you to disclose when a video is "altered or synthetic" and might be mistaken for something real. Tick the "altered or synthetic content" box on YouTube, or turn on the "AI-generated content" label on TikTok, whenever your video might be confused with reality. We don't tick those boxes for you — that's your job at upload time.

Reporting bad behaviour, and what we can do about it

If you find someone using our app to publish something that breaks this policy, email abuse@drevalis.com with the link and which rule you think they broke. We can't see anyone's installation or remove their content — but we can look into their license and cancel it if the evidence is clear. To take the actual content down, you'll need to report it to whichever platform is hosting it (YouTube, TikTok, etc.).

If we cancel a license for breaking these rules, it happens immediately, no refund. We email the account holder with the reason.

What we won't get involved with

Outside the categories above, we don't police the topics you cover. Political opinions, adult content (where the platform allows it), strong language, controversial takes — that's between you, your audience, and the platform you publish on. Not us.
