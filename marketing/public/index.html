What it does

Every step of making a video, done for you.

Set up your show once, then a new episode is three clicks: pick the show, type the topic, hit Generate.

Step 1 · Writing

Scripts that sound the same across 100 episodes.

Configure your show's tone, characters, and format once. After that, type a topic and the AI writes a full scripted episode in your show's voice — scene by scene, with consistent hooks and pacing.

  • Use any LLM. Local (LM Studio, Ollama) or cloud (Claude, OpenAI). If one's down, the next in your fallback chain picks up.
  • Long-form planned, not drifted. For 10+ minute videos the app writes a chapter outline first, then writes each chapter aware of the rest.
  • Edit any scene. Rewrite a single line, regenerate just the hook, or swap whole chapters without re-running anything else.
Step 2 · Voice

Free local voices, paid studio voices, and per-character tagging.

Hundreds of natural-sounding voices ship locally — no uploads, no per-character billing. Mix them with paid options (ElevenLabs) for studio quality. Tag a line with [Alice] or [Bob] and the matching voice picks it up — useful for dialogue and multi-character audiobooks.

  • Edge, Kokoro & Piper. All run on your machine, no internet round-trip.
  • Natural pauses. Short breaths between lines, longer ones when a new speaker enters.
  • Pitch & pace. Tweak without re-rendering the whole video.
Step 3 · Visuals

Your look, your style, scene by scene.

Drevalis ships ComfyUI workflows tuned for vertical (Shorts/TikTok) and widescreen (YouTube long-form) — or point it at your own workflow. Got multiple GPUs? The app spreads scenes across them. One scene didn't come out? Re-run only that scene.

  • Same look, every episode. Style references + character packs keep the visuals consistent.
  • Resumable. A mid-pipeline failure preserves completed scenes; hit retry and it picks up where it left off.
  • No GPU? Rent one. Point Drevalis at a RunPod / Vast.ai pod (Pro plan) and the heavy work runs on rented hardware.
Step 4 · Publish

From rendered to live on YouTube — one click.

Hook up multiple YouTube channels, schedule on a drag-and-drop calendar, and let the app retry failed uploads automatically. Or just save the MP4, thumbnail, and description to disk and post however you like.

  • Multi-channel. Different upload schedules per channel, in your time zone.
  • Title, description, tags. Generated alongside the video, ready for YouTube search.
  • Thumbnails. Pick any frame, or upload your own.

Honest first

What Drevalis doesn't do.

Cheaper to be honest now than to refund you later.

It won't check copyright for you

The AI generates whatever you ask. If something ends up resembling a copyrighted brand or character, that's on you to catch before publishing.

It's not a cloud backup

Your videos live on your computer — we never see them. The "backup to one file" button is up to you to keep somewhere safe.

It doesn't disclose AI for you

YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram each have their own rules about disclosing AI-generated content. You handle the disclosure; the app just hands the MP4 to the upload endpoint.

It stops when you cancel

7-day grace period — everything keeps working. After that, no new videos and no updates until you renew. Old videos stay on your disk forever, untouched.

Before you buy

What you should know to run it.

Drevalis is a self-hosted creator tool, not a cloud SaaS. You should be comfortable with the four points below.

You install and configure external tools

Image and video scenes require ComfyUI on your machine (free, separate install). Scripts require a local LLM (LM Studio / Ollama, free) or a cloud one (Claude / OpenAI — you provide the API key). FFmpeg and Redis ship inside the Drevalis installer; you don't manage those.

Your hardware sets the ceiling

An NVIDIA GPU with 12 GB+ VRAM keeps local ComfyUI scenes responsive. No GPU? Audiobooks and still-image Shorts still run; or rent a cloud GPU (Pro plan) and the heavy work happens on rented hardware. The app itself is light — the slow parts are whatever model you point it at.

You're responsible for what you publish

The app generates whatever you ask. Copyright, likeness rights, and YouTube/TikTok AI-disclosure rules are on you to check before posting. Drevalis hands the MP4 to the upload endpoint — it doesn't moderate the content.

Your data lives on your disk

Scripts, voices, MP4s, and channel credentials never leave your machine. Our servers only check that your subscription is active (one yes/no request per day). There's a one-file backup button — keeping that file safe is up to you.

Common questions

What kind of computer do I need?

Windows 10 (22H2+) or Windows 11, 8 GB RAM, any recent CPU. Image and video scenes want an NVIDIA GPU with 12 GB+ VRAM, or a rented cloud GPU (Pro plan). Audiobooks and still-image Shorts run fine without a GPU. See the Download page for full system requirements.

Do I need to install other software?

For scenes: ComfyUI (free, separate install). For scripts: a local LLM (LM Studio / Ollama, free) or a cloud one (Claude / OpenAI — you bring the API key). FFmpeg and Redis come bundled inside the installer.

Does any of my data get sent to you?

No. Scripts, voices, videos, and channel credentials stay on your computer. Our servers only check your subscription is active — once a day, just a yes/no. Privacy policy →

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. drevalis.com/account — change plans, switch payment method, cancel, or download invoices. After cancellation everything keeps working for 7 days, then stops issuing updates and new generations.

Is it legal to publish AI-generated videos?

Depends on jurisdiction, platform, and what's in the video. You're responsible for the disclosure and for not impersonating real people or copying others' work. Acceptable Use →
