The 2,500-Year-Old Secret of Yak Butter Tea

Series: World's Strangest Foods: Bites That Shock

--- Script ---

[Scene 1]
Yak Butter Tea, a 2,500-year-old Tibetan beverage, is essential for surviving the harsh Himalayan climate.

[Scene 2]
In 600 BCE, the Yarlung Dynasty in Tibet began trading tea from the Sichuan region, 1,000 miles away.

[Scene 3]
Yak butter, rich in fat, is blended with tea to create a high-calorie drink, perfect for high altitudes.

[Scene 4]
The tea leaves used are often from the Pemako region, known for its unique, earthy flavor.

[Scene 5]
In 1938, a British expedition found that consuming Yak Butter Tea helped prevent altitude sickness.

[Scene 6]
The tea is traditionally prepared in a large, wooden churn, which is vigorously shaken to emulsify the ingredients.

[Scene 7]
Paradoxically, this high-fat drink is believed to lower cholesterol and improve cardiovascular health.
